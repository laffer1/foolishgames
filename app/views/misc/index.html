Jewel Mailing Lists

http://www.smoe.org

This is an un-moderated discussion list, currently available as loose mail or in digest form. To subscribe (or unsubscribe) send mail to "jewel-request@smoe.org" with "(un)subscribe" in the body of the message. (That's the body, not the subject line.) Jewel is not on the mailing list and you cannot contact jewel by sending mail to the list.

To switch to the digest form, send the following three line message to "majordomo@smoe.org":

unsubscribe jewel
subscribe jewel-digest
end

There is a news ONLY list. No posting or discussing. This will include such things as media appearances, tours, lyrics, and reviews. To subscribe to the news list send e-mail to: jewel-news-request@smoe.org with "subscribe" or "unsubscribe" in the body, not the subject line.

"Jewel Mailing List Netiquette And New Member Guide"
http://www.quackquack.net/jewel/

The list provides a lot of different information. It is also a good place to discuss angel food, new music, and many other topics.

Jewel's official website also has a mailing list called JeweLink. For more information, visit http://www.jeweljk.com/ The list is strictly Jewel news and information about products available at the official Jewel store on the site. Many users visit both sites.

Meaningless Bits 'n Pieces From Me

I went to see my first Jewel concert September 5th, 1997 at Meadow Brook. (Rochester Hills, MI)  I loved the concert. Jewel played part of the concert by herself, and then brought out a band for the latter half of the concert. The opening act was not very good though. The guy was practically boo'd off the stage. Jewel came out during his performance and told us to stop as it was her friend. The crowd was really upset because the concert was supposed to start at 8PM EST and it was almost 8:45PM, and the opening act was still on. Jewel played between 9PM and 9:45PM. She performed mostly tracks off of POY, with a few " new " tracks. Jewel refused to sign autographs or talk to anyone after the concert. Many of us thought it was because the crowd upset her. Someone proposed marriage during the concert to Jewel by yelling from the cheap lawn seats. He was a bit intoxicated. She told him to "shut up and sit down because no one would want to marry you." He did just that. I went to the concert with a friend, Michelle. She did not care to much for the concert. At the time she told me she had a good time, but I found out later she was humoring me. Go figure. Jewel also told a short antedote about a high school she played in the Detroit area in the past. She said that the students thought she was Jewell, a rap group. She quickly was boo'd off stage. After the concert, several young girls were singing "Pieces of You" at the top of their lungs in the parking lot. Some stayed for the second "concert." My biggest complaint about the concert was that there were not any good t-shirts to purchase. Just two days later I went on my first date with my wife Caryn. I guess you could say that was a really good weekend for me.
